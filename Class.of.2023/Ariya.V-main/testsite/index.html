Fennec Fox

Fennec Foxes, also known as Vulpes zerda, are animals native to North African deserts. Their habitats are located throughout the Sahara Desert and prefer sandy desert resgions with desert grasses or scrub vegetation. Fennec Foxes do not pose any major threat to people or livestock however, they are important to control locusts and rodent populations. They are nocturnal creatures that avoid daytime heat of the dry desert environment and with the help of their bat-like ears, they stay cool. They also stay warm with an undercoat that insulates them during cold desert nights which are also used as protection from the daytime sun. These nocturnal creatures are very good diggers which allows them to create burrows to escape from predators and heat.

Characteristics

Fennec Foxes weigh around 0.68kg to 1.6kg with a height of 20cm. Their life span averages to 10 to 12 years. Most of them are cream coloured with black-tipped tails and have the largest ears of any member of the canid family (up to 6 inches), relative to their small size. They are omnivores, eating insects such as grasshoppers and locusts as well as small rodents, lizards, snails, birds and plant matter. These foxes dwell in small groups of up to 10 and live in burrows.

Population Risks

Despite not being endangered, Fennec Foxes are often hunted by owls (Verreaux's eagle owl), jackals and other mammals. The main threat to these foxes are humans, trapping them for exhibition or sale to tourists in North Africa. There is also a high risk of road kill in developed areas. They are typically not aggresive animals, however there has been cases of Fennec Fox attacks on people and other animals.

Communication

Fennec Foxes communicate in a variety of ways, using vocalisations, body language, scent marking and scratching. To communicate using vocalisations, they make a variety of sounds including: chriping, barking, and trills. Each type of vocalisation is used to express different emotions and meanings such as happiness or fear. Another form of communication they use includes body posture which tells us a lot about their feelings. As an example, if they tuckc their tails between their legs, it shows that they are anxious or scared.
